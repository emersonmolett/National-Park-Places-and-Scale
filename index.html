National Park Explorer

notes
The list of units in the National Park Service system (parks, but also national monuments, scenic rivers, and so forth) came from Franklin Webber's GitHub repo.
Visitors per year is the mean of 2011-2020 annual attendance figures according to the National Park Service. Points of interest came from the National Park Service's ArcGIS geocoordinate system. And landmass, or area, figures for each NPS unit came from the National Park Service's nps boundary ArcGIS dataset.

Area began as a value autopopulated by ESRI software based on WGS 1984 Web Mercator. Very special thanks to Matthew Rowbotham of the North Country Trail Association for converting these to acres in North America Albers Equal Area Conic projection. A spot-check of results show 555.200203 acres for Muir Woods National Monument by these calculations, which Wikipedia lists as 554 acres.

Thanks also to Roger Johnson (Chief Cartographer, Washington Support Office Land Resources Division, Fort Collins Technical Center, National Park Service) and Cam Peterson (GIS/Data Analyst, GEOSYS) for clarification on area.

--Paul Bernhardt, Matt Killeen, Ciera Morris, and Emerson Williams-Molett
